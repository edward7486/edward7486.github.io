My design philosophy

Throughout my design journey I’ve been exposed to all sorts of tactics and processes that are either utilized or not at all kinds of organizations. Agile UX, lean UX, user personas, JTBD (jobs-to-be-done), design sprints. These all have their place but my philosophy boils down to a few key points:

  • You can’t build a sticky product without understanding what users are going through. So I spend considerable energy and time with users throughout my design process.
  • I seek to align my cross-functional team and stakeholders with regular check-ins to both solicit feedback and ideas for my designs but also to ensure technical constraints are met and timelines are feasible.
  • Shared empathy drives winning teams. I often share research findings with my project team to drive empathy for users and align the team on my recommendations. This makes explaining design rationale and strategy easier and gets even the most hesitant teammates to understand the value of design.
